Pilot Plan

Hey Devon, Taylor — here's the campaign plan we discussed. Targeting strategy, email approach, and examples showing what we'll send to your prospects.

Pilot Structure

Free Pilot Phase 1
  • 2 qualified meetings booked at no cost
  • Goal: prove meeting quality and list accuracy
Extended Pilot Phase 2
  • Meetings 3, 4, 5: $300 each ($900 total)
  • No setup fee yet
  • Goal: give exec team enough data to see a close
Ongoing Phase 3
  • Setup fee: $2,000 (one-time)
  • Per qualified meeting: $450
  • Qualified = Director+ marketing leader at B2B software company, shows up, engages on AI Overviews / video ranking
Back of Napkin ROI Math

At $11.8K average deal, even at a conservative 1-in-5 close rate from cold outbound:

5 meetings × $300 = $1,500 + $2,000 setup = $3,500 total cost
1 closed deal = $11,800 revenue

That's about 30% CAC. Reasonable for a new channel.

If close rate improves to 2-in-5 as you refine the pitch: $3,500 ÷ $23,600 = 15% CAC.

Campaign Approach

This pilot: one campaign idea, tested properly.

Based on market research and what's already working for you (category-specific + "you're not showing up" personalization), this is the angle most likely to resonate.

We'll send to ~1,000 prospects with minor variations (subject lines, CTAs, sentence order) swapped out for deliverability. These don't impact conversion – the core message is what matters. That's what we're testing.

If it flops, we develop a new angle. Could be category takeover pitch, different pain framing, or different proof points. But no point testing 5 angles poorly. Better to test one angle properly, get data, iterate.

Email Templates

Philosophy

  • Open with specific question about their search term
  • Explain mechanism simply (analysts review on your channel, Google sources from it)
  • Personalized video idea based on their positioning
  • Proof in PS, not body
  • No links (deliverability)
  • No accusations ("you're losing traffic")

Subject lines, CTAs, and micro-wording get swapped out for deliverability. These don't impact conversion – the core message is what matters. That's what we're testing.
